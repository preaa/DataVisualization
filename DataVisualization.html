Baywheels Data Exploration and Visualization¶

by Prerna Bhand¶

Preliminary Wrangling¶

The BayWheels Dataset (Formerly called as Ford GoBike Dataset) is a collection of trips from various drivers from several years. In this report, 2019 data has been taken. The aim of this project is to explore and gain insights of the data as well as provide visualizations for clear understaing of the trends and insights.

Each trip is anonymized and includes:

  1. Trip Duration (seconds)
  2. Start Time and Date
  3. End Time and Date
  4. Start Station ID
  5. Start Station Name
  6. Start Station Latitude
  7. Start Station Longitude
  8. End Station ID
  9. End Station Name
  10. End Station Latitude
  11. End Station Longitude
  12. Bike ID
  13. User Type (Subscriber or Customer Subscriber = Member or Customer = Casual)

Following questions are interesting to explore¶

  1. What's the most popular station to pick up and return the bike?
  2. What are the ride usage patterns during the week and on weekends?
  3. What is average ride duration? How does it vary on weekdays and weekends?
  4. And finally, does user type - Customer or Subscriber have a different pattern of usage during the week.

What is the structure of your dataset?¶

The dataset contains 15 columns and 2506983 rows. The columns contain start time, end time of the ride, bike identification, coordinates of the start and end stations. There are two more columns bike_share_for_all_trip and rental_access_method.

What is/are the main feature(s) of interest in your dataset?¶

The dataset contains bike trip data between specific stations and there duration. Feature of interest in this dataset is to find out what are the most popular stations where bikes were picked or dropped off, what are the average ride durations during the week, which days of the week have higher usage or longer durations of rides. Also the user type - Customer or Subscriber and their usage pattern is interested to explore.

What features in the dataset do you think will help support your investigation into your feature(s) of interest?¶

The user_type to understand memberships, the duration and timings of rides, dates - weekday and weekend will help investigate our features of interest in the dataset

For subscribers, the figure does not vary so much, but still higher ride durations on Weekends. We could attribute this to a possibility that Customers tends to use BayWheels bikes for Leisure activities more as compared to Subscribers and these Leisure rides tend to be higher in duration as compared to Subscribers. Subscribes are using BayWheels consistently for their work trips as well as leisure trips, but the durations doe not vary so much.

The interesting part in this visualization is the user behaviour. Customers have quite high ride duration during the weekend and seems their bike rides are for leisure activities. Subscribers ride durations are not varying that much, indicating they tend to ride BayWheels for both purposes - work trips and leisure, but do not engage in higher durations leisure rides on weekend as much as Customers.
